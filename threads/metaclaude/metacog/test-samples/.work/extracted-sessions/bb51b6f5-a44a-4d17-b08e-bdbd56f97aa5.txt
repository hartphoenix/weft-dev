Key insight: the 800 chars/chunk payload budget is unnecessarily conservative.

All models in test matrix (Qwen3-4B 32K ctx, Qwen3-8B 32K, Gemma-3-12B 128K)

and the metacognitive task (pattern-match + decide) is well within 4B capability
at 5K tokens. Raising to 2000 chars/chunk eliminates the embedding/retrieval
mismatch entirely (what's embedded = what's delivered).

Round 2 tests two strategies at 2000-char delivery with no truncation,
then uses subagent evaluation to assess **reasoning relevance** — not
just cosine similarity, but whether retrieved chunks would actually help
a meta-agent make useful observations.
